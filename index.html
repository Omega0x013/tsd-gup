Gup Forms & One Steps

for DBBAI Tang Soo Do.

My notes on the moves of and how to perform the forms and hand, feet, and self defence techniques I've needed to grade in Tang Soo Do.

The contents section lists everything you'll need for your grading, grouped by the grade you're going for, rather than the belt you're currently wearing.

Example: A green belt is grading to green tag and an orange tag is double grading to green tag. They would both find the forms and one steps they're looking for marked under 'Green Tag'.

Pyung Ahn Sam Dan

The Inside-Outside Block + Low Punch happen at the same time. To set up for it:
  • Bring your feet together so they are touching.
  • Bend your knees slightly.
  • Couch your punching fist against your ribs as you normally would.
  • Bring your blocking fist across your body to your hip on the same side as your punching fist.
  • Twist your upper body 45° in the direction of your punching fist.
Then, all you need to do is throw the block and punch simultaneously, bringing your upper body back to the center to generate power.
  1. ⠒ CChoon Be Jah Se
  2. ← B Inside-Outside Block
  3. ⠒ SRight Inside-Outside Block + Left Low Punch
  4. ⠒ SLeft Inside-Outside Block + Right Low Punch
  5. ↷ B Inside-Outside Block
  6. ⠒ SLeft Inside-Outside Block + Right Low Punch
  7. ⠒ SRight Inside-Outside Block + Left Low Punch

  9. ↑ F Low Supported Spear Hand
  10. ↬ H Back Fist
  11. ← F Center Punch

Naihanchi Cho Dan

Hands

Your partner attacks; you must defend yourself using the hand technique. Make sure to Ki Hap on the final move.

Hands 1-5

  1. Elbow Strike
    Attack: High Punch
    Defence:
    1. Step left leg back into horseriding stance.
    2. Outside-in block with right hand.
    3. Supported right elbow strike to the solar plexus.
  2. Reverse Elbow Strike
    Attack: High Punch
    Defence:
    1. Step left leg back into horseriding stance.
    2. Outside-in block with right hand.
    3. Supported right elbow strike to the solar plexus.
    4. Twist your upper body to left elbow strike the solar plexus.
