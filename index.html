Gup Forms & One Steps

for DBBAI Tang Soo Do.

My notes on the moves of and how to perform the forms and hand, feet, and self defence techniques I've needed to grade in Tang Soo Do.

The contents section lists everything you'll need for your grading, grouped by the grade you're going for, rather than the belt you're currently wearing.

Example: A green belt is grading to green tag and an orange tag is double grading to green tag. They would both find the forms and one steps they're looking for marked under 'Green Tag'.

Forms

Key

Accompanying each move is two symbols: one for the direction you are stepping and one for the stance you end up in.

Direction Symbols

  • · - Not Moving.
  • ⠒ - Both feet together.
  • ↑ - Straight ahead.
  • ← - 90° to the left.
  • → - 90° to the right.
  • ↶ - 180° to the left.
  • ↷ - 180° to the right.
  • ↖ - 45° to the left.
  • ↗ - 45° to the right.
  • ↫ - 270° to the left.
  • ↬ - 270° to the right.
  • ↙ - 135° to the left.
  • ↘ - 135° to the right.
  • ↓ - Stepping backwards.
These directions are relative to the direction your chest is facing at the end of the previous move. For most moves in Horseriding Stance, the directions of your chest and head are not aligned.

Stance Symbols

  • C - Choon Be (Ready Stance).
  • S - Standing.
  • F - Front Stance.
  • B - Back Stance.
  • H - Horseriding Stance.
  • ∗ - Stances with a border around them are form-specific.
If you're not already familiar with the stances, then this resource will be dibsicult to parse. I strongly recommend asking your instructor to show you the stances in person.

Horizontal Lines

The horizontal lines indicate a place where you should Ki Hap. They often represent natural breaks in the form, and may be useful for teaching forms in stages.

Pyung Ahn Ee Dan

The Inside-Outside Block + Overhead Block in this form is special. You throw the two blocks simultaneously beside and above your head so that your arms form the outline of a square. Your fists should be turned outwards, so that your knuckles are facing the front.

When transitioning from the ·  B Supported Upper Cut to the ·  H Center Punch, bring your front foot up to your back knee before stepping out into Horseriding Stance.

In the Side Kick + Back Fist, make sure your arm and leg are parallel to each other, even if that means having them both point toward the ground.

Pyung Ahn Sam Dan

The Inside-Outside Block + Low Punch happen at the same time. To set up for it:
  • Bring your feet together so they are touching.
  • Bend your knees slightly.
  • Couch your punching fist against your ribs as you normally would.
  • Bring your blocking fist across your body to your hip on the same side as your punching fist.
  • Twist your upper body 45° in the direction of your punching fist.
Then, all you need to do is throw the block and punch simultaneously, bringing your upper body back to the center to generate power.

Hands

You begin in the starting stance; your partner begins in a front stance low block. Your partner attacks; you must defend yourself using the foot technique.

Hands 1-5

Starting Stance: Choon Be Jah Se
Attack: High Punch
  1. Elbow Strike - ↗ H Step out to the right into Horseriding Stance, performing an Outside-Inside Knife Hand Block to the attacking arm. Perform a supported right elbow strike to the solar plexus.
  2. Elbow Strike & Reverse Elbow Strike - ↗ H Stepping out to the right, perform an Outside-Inside Knife Hand Block. Perform a supported right elbow strike, then pivot anticlockwise to perform a left elbow strike to the solar plexus.
  3. Knife Hand Strike - ↗ H Stepping out to the right, perform an Outside-Inside Knife Hand Block. Using the same hand, perform a knife hand strike to your partner's throat.
  4. Reverse Punch - ↗ H Stepping out to the right, perform an Outside-Inside Knife Hand Block. ·  F Twist into front stance and perform a reverse punch to the solar plexus.
  5. Block + Punch - ↗ H Stepping out to the right, simultaneously perform and Inside-Outside Knife Hand Block with your left hand, and a punch the solar plexus with your right hand.

Hands 6-10

Starting Stance: Choon Be Jah Se
Attack: High Punch
  1. Triple Punch - ↖ H Stepping out to the left, perform an Outside-Inside Knife Hand Block with your left hand. Perform two punches to the solar plexus. ·  F Twist into front stance and perform a punch to your partner's head.
  2. Block + Knife Hand Strike - ↗ H Stepping out to the right, simultaneously perform and Inside-Outside Knife Hand Block with your left hand, and Knife Hand Strike to your partner's throat.
  3. Grab & Elbow Strike - ·  B Step back with your right foot and perform an Inside-Outside Knife Hand Block with your left hand. Grab your partner's arm, twist it anticlockwise and pull them towards you. Perform a right elbow strike to your partner's face.
  4. Elbow Strike & Backfist & Hammer Fist - ↗ H Stepping out to the right, perform an Outside-Inside Knife Hand Block. Perform a supported right elbow strike, then perform a backfist to the face, then a hammer fist to the groin.
  5. Punch & Block + Punch - ↗ H Steping out to the right, perform a left punch to the solar plexus. Simultaneously perform an Inside-Outside Knife Hand Block with your left hand, and a punch to the solar plexus with your right hand.

Feet

You begin in the starting stance; your partner begins in a front stance low block. Your partner attacks; you must defend yourself using the foot technique.
For foot techniques that involve a side kick, you may want to kick into the empty space underneath your partner's arm, past their ribs, to avoid injuring them.

Feet 1-5

Starting Stance: Right Leg Back; Free Fighting Position
Attack: High Punch
  1. Front Kick - ·  B Perform a front kick to the center of your partner's body.
  2. Side Kick - ·  B Perform a side kick to the center of your partner's body.
  3. Round Kick - ·  B Perform a round kick your partner's ribs.
  4. Back Kick - ·  B Perform a back kick to the center of your partner's body.
  5. Duck & Side Kick - ↖ H duck your head and step out to the left with your left foot, then perform a side kick to the center of your partner's body.

Feet 6-10

Starting Stance: Right Leg Back; Free Fighting Position
Attack: High Punch
  1. Duck & Round Kick - ↖ H duck your head and step out to the left with your left foot, then perform a round kick to your partner's ribs.
  2. Push Kick - ·  B Perform a push kick to your partner's chest. First make contact with the blade of your foot, then push them back.
  3. Wrist Grab & Round Kick - ·  B Perform an Inside-Outside Knife Block, then grab your partner's wrist and perform a round kick to their ribs.
  4. Wrist Grab & Side Kick - ·  B Perform an Inside-Outside Knife Block, then grab your partner's wrist and perform a side kick the center of their body.
  5. Wrist Grab & Hook Kick - ↖ H Step out to the left with your left foot, performing an Inside-Outside Knife Block with your left hand. Grab your partner's wrist and perform a hook kick to their back using your right foot.

Self Defence

Your partner grabs you; you must then escape using the self defence technique.

Self Defence 1-5

  1. Same Side Wrist Grab
    Attack: Partner grabs your left wrist with their right hand.
    Defence: ↗ H Stepping back with your right foot into Horseriding Stance, twist and pull your wrist down and away from your partner's hand to break their grip. Perform a Left Knife Hand Strike to their throat.
  2. Cross Over Wrist Grab
    Attack: Partner grabs your right wrist with their right hand.
    Defence: ↗ H Stepping back with your right foot, perform a Left Knife Hand Strike to your partner's wrist, breaking their grip. Pull your hand away and perform a Right Knife Hand Strike to their throat.
  3. Shoulder Grab
    Attack: Partner grabs your left shoulder with their right hand.
    Defence: ⠒ S Bring your left arm up and over your partner's arm, hooking your arm around their elbow. ↑ F Stepping forward into a high front stance, use your right arm to push upward on your partner's arm, locking their elbow.
  4. Shirt Grab
    Attack: Partner grabs your left shirt or lapel with their right hand.
    Defence: ⠒ S Grab your partner's hand with your right hand, placing your thumb on the back of their hand. ↗ H Stepping back with your right foot, perform a Left Knife Hand Strike to your partner's elbow, and drag their hand and arm down toward the ground.
  5. Belt Grab
    Attack: Partner grabs your belt from underneath using their right hand.
    Defence: ↑ F Stepping forward with your left foot, grab your partner's elbow with both hands and pull towards your chest, locking their elbow.

Self Defence 6-10

For Self Defence 9 and 10, you don't have to take your partner all the way down. Pushing them far enough that they have to hold onto you to stay upright is sufficient.
  1. Rear Belt Grab
    Attack: Partner stands behind and to the left of you. They grab the back of your belt with their right hand.
    Defence: ⠒ S Twist your upper body and grab your partner's shoulder with your left hand and their elbow with your right. ↗ F Stepping forward with your left leg, push on your partner's shoulder and elbow, forcing them toward the floor.
  2. Rear Collar Grab
    Attack: Partner stands behind and to the left of you. They grab the back of your collar with their right hand.
    Defence: ⠒ S Grab your partner's elbow with your left hand and their hand with your right hand. ↗ F Stepping forward with your left foot, push on their elbow and pull on their hand to force them toward the floor.
  3. Double Wrist Grab
    Attack: Partner stands behind and to the left of you. They grab your left wrist with their left hand, and your right wrist with their right hand, pinning it behind your back.
    Defence: ↖ F Stepping back with your left foot, twist and pull your left wrist away from your partner's arm, breaking their grip. ↫ H Moving your left foot, pivot around to face your partner, turning your hand to grab their wrist. Pin your partner's wrist behind their back using your right hand and grab their shoulder with your left hand. Pull both toward you to pull them off balance.
  4. Bear Hug
    Attack: Partner stands on your left and hugs you around your shoulders with their right arm.
  5. Head Lock
    Attack: Partner stands on your left and puts their right arm around your kneck, dragging you down to their waist height. They grab their own right wrist with their left hand.
